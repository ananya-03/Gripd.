# Gripd. — Product Requirements Document
**Version:** 0.1 | **Type:** Hackathon Build | **Platform:** Mobile PWA

---

> *"What if your commute was your most productive hour?"*
> The learning game built for the platform we all hate — the MRT.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

### The FOMO Trap
Say yes to social → rush to MRT → come home too exhausted to learn. Say no → have FOMO and still don't learn. There's no winning.

### Dead Commute Time
35–50 minutes each way on the MRT. One hand on a pole, noisy, can't type. That's 70–100 minutes per day completely wasted.

### The Guilt Spiral
AI papers pile up. GitHub stars accumulate. Hackathons pass. You fall behind. You feel bad. Repeat.

**Gripd. doesn't fight your social life — it turns the unavoidable commute into the learning session.**

---

## 2. Vision

A mobile-first, voice-native, gamified learning app that converts passive commute time into active skill-building. Users save content (papers, repos, events) during their day, and the app serves it back as interactive games and AI voice narration during their MRT ride — one-handed, no typing required.

**Core insight:** You're not too lazy to learn. You're just too tired and unstimulated by the time you get home. If an AI narrates your saved papers on the MRT like an excited podcast host, you exit the train *wanting* to go home and build.

---

## 3. Target User

- Tech-curious professionals / students in Singapore
- Night owls: active social life, late nights, rushed mornings
- MRT commuters with 30–50 min rides
- People with a backlog of unread papers, unvisited repos, unmissed hackathons
- Builders who feel guilty about not self-improving

## 4. Core Modes

### 🎙️ Voice Hype Mode *(Signature Feature)*
The moment you board, Gemini Live narrates your saved content — not a boring summary, but an excited, opinionated explainer that makes you want to go home and build something. Works hands-free. You can ask "wait, explain that again" mid-ride. Pre-cached for offline tunnel use.

- Powered by **Gemini flash-3.1-live** for real-time voice dialogue
- High-quality offline TTS via **ElevenLabs** (pre-cached at home)
- Content sourced from **Exa.ai** neural search on your saved URLs

### 🏗️ Build-a-System
You're given a real-world engineering scenario ("Twitter just got 10x traffic"). Drag and tap architecture components — load balancer, cache, DB replica — onto a canvas to design the system. The AI evaluates your choices and explains tradeoffs. No typing. Tap-based.

- Feedback engine: **GPT-5.5**
- Scenario generation and evaluation from saved content context

### ⚔️ Paper vs Paper
Two AI papers go head to head. You swipe left/right on claims — which approach wins on efficiency, generalisability, compute cost? The game reveals actual benchmark results after each round. Feels like a card battle, teaches you real research tradeoffs.

- Analysis engine: **GPT-5.5**
- Papers sourced and matched via **Exa.ai**
- Illustrated cards generated by **GPT Image 2**

### 🌌 Concept Galaxy
A 3D node graph of your knowledge, built from your saved content. Each node is a concept. Tap to explore connections. Your job is to discover links between ideas — not answer questions. Exploration-driven, curiosity-rewarded.

- Node visuals: **Fal.ai** real-time generation
- Knowledge graph built from content via **Exa.ai**

### 🔥 Hot Take Reactor
The AI drops a spicy tech opinion ("RAG is dead, just fine-tune"). You argue for or against using what you've learned. The AI debates back. Your reasoning is scored — not your answer. Voice or tap.

- Voice debate: **Gemini flash-3.1-live**
- Shareable clip of your best argument (social feature)

## 5. UX Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|---|---|
| **One thumb** | All interactions are swipe, tap, or voice. Zero typing while commuting. |
| **10-min chunks** | Every game round fits between two stops. Auto-saves state if you overshoot. |
| **Offline ready** | Content pre-cached at home via background sync. Works in MRT tunnels. |
| **XP + streaks** | Skill XP per session. Streak bonuses for consecutive commutes. Level up in Systems Design, ML, DevOps etc. |
| **Share clips** | Hot Take debates can be clipped and shared — your commute becomes content. |
| **No guilt UX** | No empty states that make you feel behind. The app meets you where you are. |

## 8. MVP Scope (1 Hackathon Day)

Ship these five things. Nothing else.

1. **Save a link** — paste any ArXiv or GitHub URL. Exa.ai fetches and indexes it automatically.
2. **Voice Hype** — Gemini Live narrates your saved content as an exciting explainer. This is the demo hook.
3. **One game mode** — Ship Paper vs Paper or Build-a-System fully. Show the swipe/tap UX working end to end.
4. **XP + level** — Basic skill progression so the demo shows growth over a simulated session.
5. **PWA install prompt** — Works on a phone home screen. Judges can install and try it live.
